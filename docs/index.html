Why This Matters Now

In Kenya and East Africa, black African queer females, trans and gender-queer folks, and survivors of sexual and gender-based violence face intersecting crises:

Digital Tracking & Control

Ex-partners use mobile network location services to monitor movements for months. Family members snoop on phones to control communication and work. Screenshots and leaked data are weaponized for blackmail and outing.

Banking Exclusion

M-Pesa and bank accounts freeze when ID names don't match gender expression. Trans and non-binary folks are denied access to formal financial services. Sex workers and informal income earners are marked as "suspicious activity."

Criminalization & Custody Threats

Sexuality, gender identity, and survival work are criminalized. Family members file custody cases to separate queer parents from children. Police involvement looms if identities are exposed.

Erasure & Inauthenticity

Existing platforms require "code-switching"—adopting non-authentic personas for safety. There are almost no digital spaces where queer African women can be fully seen, heard, held, and authentic.

These are not hypothetical problems. They are the daily reality for our community.

The House of Bijou Platform

A stealth-by-design app that masquerades as a benign utility while providing layered safety, community, and economic tools.

Stealth Mode

Launches as a calculator, weather app, or game. Secret gesture/PIN reveals real community dashboard. If your phone is checked, no one knows what's inside.

Panic Alert

One shake, gesture, or button press silently alerts 3–5 trusted contacts with your location. Community emergency responders mobilize in minutes.

Mutual Check-Ins

3-person tracking system. You keep tabs on peers when moving around, they keep tabs on you. No central surveillance. Pure peer protection.

Encrypted Chat

End-to-end encrypted messages. Delete-on-timer. No server logs. Discuss safety, resistance strategies, chosen family—with zero traces.

Low-Trace Transfers

Send and receive money peer-to-peer without a bank account. Works on feature phones (USSD). Encrypted ledger. Hard to freeze, hard to trace.

Mutual Aid

Transparent community resource pooling. Need emergency funds? Post a request. Community responds within 24 hours. Radical solidarity, recorded trust.

Built Right, Built Differently

House of Bijou runs on a fully open-source, feminist-friendly tech stack designed for accessibility, privacy, and team velocity.

Rails + PostgreSQL

Why: Fast iteration, built-in encryption, real-time capability. One developer can ship features in days, not months.

Hotwire + Tailwind

Why: Low bandwidth, accessible, works on slow connections. 70% less data than React.

Turbo Native

Why: One Rails app = web + Android + iOS. Instant updates, no App Store delays. Security fixes deploy in minutes.

USSD + SMS

Why: Works on any phone, any connectivity. Feature phone users, offline users, all included.

AES-256 + Signal Protocol

Why: Everything sensitive encrypted. No cloud logging. Even if servers are hacked, user data stays safe.

Local-First

Why: All sensitive data stays on your device. Synced to server only if you want. Maximum control.

Cost to run: $50–100/month. Scales to 50,000+ users without significant infrastructure increase.

Economic Freedom Is the Foundation

Safety without economic independence is incomplete. House of Bijou is designed to build economic power at every stage.

1

Emergency Survival

Community panic fund (emergency loans, 0% interest). Rapid mutual aid (24-hour mobilization). Goal: Survive immediate crisis without exploitation.

2

Sustainable Income

Low-trace peer transfers. Transparent mutual aid ledger (builds community credit history). Survival income resources (sex work, gig work, crafts—safely).

3

Economic Autonomy

App ledger as proof of "income" for microloans. Financial literacy workshops. Blockchain wallet (Year 2) for censorship-resistant funds.

4

Collective Power

Track aggregate resource flows for movement-building. Policy advocacy ("We moved X million KES this year"). Build cooperative/social enterprise from community fund.

The Revenue Model Aligns With Community: 1–2% peer transfer fee funds hosting + USSD + moderation. Community wealth-building, not extraction.
